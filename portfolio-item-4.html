Gator Grub Restaurant-Ordering Website

Done Using Python, Flask, Bootstrap, SQL, HTML, JavaScript, and CSS

For this project, I have done this in a team of 6 for my last semester attending SFSU for a software engineering course. Our main task was to behave alike a real, legitimate company, where our instructor would act as the CEO, and his his partner (my previous instructor from my web development course), the CTO. Our team was divided up into roles: 1 person would be the team lead, 1 person would be the front-end lead, 1 would be a front-end member, 1 would be a back-end lead, 1 would be a back-end member, and the last would be the Github master (the one responsible for keeping our work organized on Github and adding changes to the repositories if necessary). I was the front-end lead.

For our project, we have done a restaurant website called Gator Grub for our college. In this website, we decided to use Python, Flask, Bootstrap, SQL, HTML, JavaScript, and CSS, with Linux being our platform to work on. As the front-end lead, I dealt the most with doing the HTML, JavaScript, and CSS of Gator Grub using Flask imports (with some assistance from my front-end member). Our backend dealt with creating our restaurant website server on AWS and doing the SQL queries while making updates to the Python code we all worked on together to link our front-end work to the queries. My team lead contributed by adding the Bootstrap template to our website, while our Github master assisted with any issues we had with pushing our work to the repositories on Github.

To describe the purpose of this website, it was to allow for customers to be able to search for any restaurants nearby campus, and allowing for him/her to be able to make orders from their page through Gator Grub. We have enabled several functionalities for our website, such as adding Google Maps for the customer to locate the restaurant, adding an orders page that carries all of the customer's orders (including if he/she wishes to make any adjustments to an order), allowing the customer to be able to search for restaurants through a nav bar, and to be able to log in and out of their account.

For this restaurant website, we have not just included functionality for the customer, but also for delivery people and new aspiring managers wishing to put their restaurants up on Gator Grub so that it would be available to seeking customers looking for a restaurant in the website. For these kinds of things, we have included pages for delivery people to check up on any order they receive from customers if they chose for delivery so that they may transport the food from that specific restaruant to their location. We have also included pages for managers of restaurants to be able to update their restaurant's information, and if whether or not they wish to take it off from Gator Grub.
